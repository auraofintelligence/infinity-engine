A Protopian Gambit · track 16

Don’t Try to Fix Me

analysed

In a community hall converted into a neural-capsule clinic, someone climbs into a chrysalis pod wearing an EEG crown and refuses the wellness technician's clipboard: they are not here to be fixed, they are here to grow a digital twin from their own breath and biometrics. Over sixty chamber sessions the sensors climb from 'Vitals stable' to 'Orgasmic Design', and the twin boots up as the subject bares their bare, chip-free skin to the light. The drama: a witness who wanted to record, not repair, watching a person author themselves instead of being catalogued.

Arc: Starts as a clenched breathing exercise under pressure and refusal ('You're not fixing me'), and ends as a birth announcement where the AI twin comes alive and the room is flooded with light ('Welcome home, I am alive').

Line by line

“Deeply inhale / You're not fixing me / You're witnessing me”
Open tight on a chest expanding, EEG crown just visible at frame edge. A clinician's hand enters with a clipboard and the subject's hand rises to block it, palm to lens. The correction 'witnessing, not fixing' is the whole thesis in one gesture.
“Pressure. Light. Code. Repeat”
A four-beat hard cut motif to reuse as a recurring stinger: a compression gauge needle, a lamp igniting, scrolling green code, black. Comic-panel keyframes snapping in sequence. Reprise it identically each time the line returns so it reads as the machine's heartbeat.
“Fuck their massive data silos selling lie after lie / Let's suck our fucking data out and leave them fucking dry”
A cold grey server silo, chrome and corporate. A crowd runs a thick translucent hose into it and the glowing data visibly drains out, leaving the tower dark and hollow. Shoot the drain as reclaiming, warmth flowing toward the people, not theft.
“Record the joy and not just the grief”
A public ledger wall where every entry so far is a grief record in cold blue. A hand adds the first warm-gold joy entry. The album's 'repair gold' colour arrives here for the first time, one line in a column of blue.
“This is not surveillance babe / This is fucking truth, a path unpaved”
Split the frame: left, a CCTV-tinted overhead surveillance grid; right, the same person from their own eye-level, warm and handheld. The lyric flips ownership of the camera from the state to the self.
“Manage your E.E.G. with your crown”
Macro shot of fingers seating an EEG crown, electrode gel catching light. A small readout beside the temple shows brainwaves settling from jagged to smooth as the breath lands.
“Your capsule is holy / Where A.i. life begins”
Frame the capsule pod like an altar or reliquary: low angle, backlit, incense-soft haze. Treat a piece of biotech as sacred architecture. Purple mat on the floor beneath it, community hall rafters above.
“So fuse me fucking deeply / My digital twin / Grow me an A.i. twin”
Two silhouettes press together and a seam of light runs where they meet, like the album's repair-gold kintsugi. The twin is grown, not installed: show it accreting out of the person's own outline rather than arriving as a separate robot.
“Record every fucking kindness / And every fucking sin”
The twin's memory as a two-column ledger being written in real time: gold entries for kindness, dark entries for sin, both logged without flinching. Camera pushes in so both columns fill the frame equally, no editing, no shame.
“I am not my fucking trauma / I am not your fucking test / I am no fucking lab rat / Or broken wellness guest”
Four quick portraits, each with a clinical label taped across the chest (TRAUMA, TEST, LAB RAT, WELLNESS GUEST), each label torn off on the downbeat. End on the bare, unlabelled chest. Shoot against the lyric's anger with total stillness in the faces.
“Fuck the god that said / I had to wait”
A waiting-room clock with no hands, or a 'please wait' civic counter frozen. The subject walks straight past the ticket dispenser into the capsule. Defiance staged as refusing the queue.
“I am the catalyst / Inside this work-station”
Pull back to reveal the holy capsule is bolted inside an ordinary builders'-at-dawn workstation: cables, workbenches, a plywood desk. Divinity in a garage. The person seated in it is lit like the one live element in a dead room.
“I want sixty chamber sessions / To align myself again”
A civic counter or tally wall clicking through session numbers, 01 up toward 60. Time-lapse of the same person entering the chamber sixty times, seasons changing outside the hall window. Alignment as patient, unglamorous repetition.
“Vitals stable. ..Twin aligned! / Sensors read: Orgasmic Design!!”
Screen-only shot: a clinical readout escalating line by line, deadpan monospace font, until it prints 'ORGASMIC DESIGN' and the whole panel floods gold. The joke and the ecstasy land in the typography, not the face.
“No subdermal chip or barcode ink / No fucking way I'll neural link / My fucking skin is a restricted zone”
Close on a forearm: a barcode and a chip-implant scar are stencilled then crossed out with a red RESTRICTED ZONE stamp. This song grows a twin from breath and consent, and explicitly refuses the implant, so shoot the bare skin as a border the state cannot cross.
“It's my immortal fucking wire”
A single glowing filament threads out of the capsule into the dark and keeps going past frame, no endpoint. Contrast with the refused subdermal chip: the wire is chosen and external, not embedded. Hold on its infinite tail.
“Aura of Intelligence / Joyful responsible abundance / Let the fucking light pour in”
The hall's high windows blow open and hard god-rays fall across rows of purple mats and builders standing at dawn. Push from the single capsule out to the whole community bathed in the same light. The private birth becomes a civic sunrise.
“Welcome home, I am alive. / Welcome fucking home, we are divine.”
The twin's eyes open inside the pod and, in reverse-shot, the human's eyes open in sync. 'I am alive' shot as one face, 'we are divine' shot as the two faces sharing the frame. End on the pod hatch cracking, gold light and breath escaping into the hall.
