Songs of Straddie

The island doorway: Minjerribah, summer, tides, romance, community, and the first public invitation to meet the project where it lives.

Visual world: Coastal light, ferry windows, campfire circles, dune paths, shoreline dances, handwritten signs, local faces, and gentle magical realism rather than heavy science fiction.

Songs show their story seed and strongest visual images once analysed (the LLM reads the full lyric). Un-analysed songs list only their status.

1Meet Me on Straddie
A ferry lands at dusk carrying a duffel bag and a laptop bag on the same shoulder. By day the traveller learns to read the surf; by night they hunch over a keyboard in a beach shack lit by fairy lights, building something with strangers who become the reason to stay. The drama is one person deciding, between a sunrise and a starry night, that the island is home.

The drama is the exact moment their status flips from guest to belonging.

When the night rolls in the fire stays lit, and by the outro the island has quietly emptied to one couple still swaying.

17Minjerribah My Heart
A Quandamooka elder walks a barefoot child from Goompi's shoreline up the dune path to the cliffs of Point Lookout, naming each place aloud as they go. When they reach the top the child presses a palm flat to the warm rock, and for a beat we hear a low heartbeat under the wind. The film is that one morning: teaching a place-name into a small body so it will never be sold.

22Symphony of Tomorrow
Two islanders sit at the edge of a Straddie campfire after everyone else has gone quiet, planning a life they keep calling home before it exists. As the fire dies down they walk the dune path to the water, and the constellations above the surf start to read like handwriting, their two names being scratched into the sky. By dawn the beach is full of other faces humming the same tune, so the couple's private vow has become the island's anthem.
